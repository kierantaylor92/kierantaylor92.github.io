Jetstar My Account

Introducing a fresh new member experience for Jetstar travellers

●2019 / isobar

Project overview

Jetstar My account needed a fresh space for our members to see when they log in. After being on a legacy platform it was needed to revitalise the look and feel to be consistent with how the site had evolved and to address the core functionalities and navigation which were unclear and confusing. My role was to revisit previous blue sky concepts and treejack testing that had been done and consider how it could kickstart the MVP for the new My Account, including a new IA that considered the future state, and the layout needed to evolve to display future content.

Setting up My Account

I took the designs from mid-fi wireframes through to finished UI including developer handoff, leading weekly checkins with various Product Owners as well as rationalising the designs to our UX design team so that it could be picked up and evolved by other tribes in the future and providing suggestions on potential features which could be incorporated.

A fresh space for when our members log in

We landed on the concept of a dashboard for users to see when they login. This would allow quick navigation between sections of their account and include space to display Club Jetstar savings widgets, price watch features and notifications as well as the possibility of gamifying the dash, which I used to attempt introducing out mascot ‘Jetta’ into the Australian market.
The key sections within the MVP were broken up into a users personal details, their preferred payment methods and a specific Club Jetstar section. It was important to take into consideration future state when deciding on these as to plan the IA for any expected features to fit in as well.
If a user had an upcoming trip we were also able to display that within My Account and link through to the corresponding 'Manage My Booking', I noted that the navigation was inconsitent between these and other parts of the site and we agreed to revisit post MVP.

Future state Jetta

We discussed some fun concepts for the squad who would pick up My Account post MVP, such as gamifying the experience and adding things to make it a bit more playful and personal. This included:
Alerting users to renew their Club Jetstar membership once expiration was approaching, for users who did not want to setup auto renew
Adding an idicator for how much details they have added - this would also promote features that most people were unaware of but quite useful, such as adding travel companions.
Updating users personal photo from having just an image of initials or photos from their social login, to preselected Jetta stickers or their own photos.

Personalised content and features to enhance the membership experience

The dashboard approach helped keep the designs relevant to both standard and Club Jetstar users. We wanted a space for CJ users to see immediate value when they logged in but also have a dedicated space to manage their membership details and payment information and this allowed us to do both.
In addition to that, we could more strategically promote these benefits to non-members by displaying it as a premium feature within their account, and more closely tying it to your overall Jetstar experience, as well as eventually adding features such as price watch and notifications.

Setting up the user journeys

As certain sections would still live on the Skysales platform until it was all migrated across, we needed a technically feasible, but logical way to direct users there. Considering the lack of a mobile breakpoint and not having the ability to modify the legacy pages, we were quite limited and opted to just make it clear when leaving the new platform, and to keep all external links together as to not confuse users between the primary navigation and legacy items. User flows for mobile, desktop and app users had to be considered which meant we needed to workshop solutions with our dedicated app team.
