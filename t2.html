Info Work

T2 redesign

● 2020 / ISOBAR

Goals

At the very beginning of our first lockdown of the pandemic, I had the opportunity right away to work on a full redesign of the T2 website along with our now fully remote team of Perth and Melbourne based designers. We were tasked with the job of giving the site a visual rehaul and aligning better with the offline brand, showcase the excellent product photography and redesign the key product pages on the website. We worked tirelessly over a period of a couple of months to smash out a huge library of pages, components and flows while navigating the new ways of working.

Process

My role as visual designer on the project was to design key pages and interactions, defining the interactions and look of new components and styles that would be added to the design system. Primarily I focused on product pages, account, checkout flows, carts and interior pages. We moved at a lightning quick pace to get through the amount of work we needed to, so we worked closely between UX and UI to establish wireframes and designs especially at the early stages to form the initial pages.

Goals

We set up 8am (10am EST) standups to maintain visibility of who was working on what which was a key part of our process and making sure we were on track. It was also our first full project working in Figma, and we were able to introduce lots of methods to streamline collaboration and processes, including design system setup and prototyping key flows. We were able to deliver screens for each page template at different breakpoints, prototypes of key flows and a design system by the end of the project
