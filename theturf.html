The Turf

● 2017 / FREELANCE

Overview

Since 2013, The Turf has focused on sharing stories from the terraces in Melbourne to the beaches of Rio de Janeiro. Taking a fresh approach to the game, the aim was to produce a fresh perspective for Australia’s youth football movement.This project was a new phase designed to evolve The Turf to reflect the growth of the last few years for the brand, we wanted to be able to include new categories such as sneakers, gaming and lifestyle that often cross over with the target market.

Evolving the brand, focusing on our strengths and including new subjects.

The new navigation aimed to provide an easier way for our users to navigate. Weak points I identified were the ability to search for content, in particular some of the higher quality articles such as exclusive player interviews and galleries. It was important to showcase them in a prominent location as well as rethinking the menu structure.

Promoting our best content front and centre.

Ensuring premium content would always be displayed, the top of the page contained a featured article to be updated regularly and promoteour best work, as well as the latest news in the football world, below that a trending section of featured, high quality articles. This new structure would allow new users to see what we were about right away and encourage exploration better than previously.Other points of emphasis were to still promote the social media avenues which is crucial for our market of young fans and call to action for anyone wanting to join the team in a variety of roles.

Framing the action. The new Turf brand.

The idea behind the visual design is the concept of framing the action, representative of our focus on everything about the game. Utilising the outlined turf logo which both increases brand awareness and make high quality photography the hero. Ideally our strong, vibrant colours are brought out contrasting with flat blacks of the photography.With a range of new categories we wanted to split up, the colour scheme was expanded and carefully selected to feel cohesive, this was used to display each of the new areas covered on the site, and allow users to recognise what is now offered.

Streamlining social.

To strengthen THE TURF brand and remain consistent, a range of social media templates can be made and used to speed up process. These will provide options that work with different images and colours, allowing us to utilise the brand colours and brand photography while maintaining legibility and being on brand. We also broadened the colour palette, to include a range that will categorise the expanded list of topics covered.
